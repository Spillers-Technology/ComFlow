Straight answers

The questions a first customer should ask.

A voicemail product handles real calls and real data. The useful answer is usually more specific than “trust us.”

Why not just use ordinary voicemail?

Ordinary voicemail captures audio. ComFlow turns that audio into a reviewable work item: transcript, callback number, reason for calling, urgency, summary, status, and notes in one inbox.

If you rarely miss calls and do not mind listening through messages, keep ordinary voicemail and keep the $9. ComFlow earns its fee when deciding and following up has become work.

Is my call data safe?

Call data is sensitive. Hosted ComFlow uses signed-in accounts and separates each customer's users, numbers, and voicemails. It stores recordings and transcripts so you can play, search, and review them; we do not pretend that storing data creates zero risk.

If you need to control the storage and service providers yourself, ComFlow can be self-hosted free. Ask the team about your retention or regulatory requirements before forwarding a line that receives sensitive calls.

What happens if I cancel?

Your plan stays active through the period you have paid for. After it ends, your account and existing voicemails remain readable; new calls and number provisioning pause.

Remove forwarding from your original line before service ends, and talk with the team in advance about any ComFlow number you want to keep.

Does ComFlow talk to callers like an AI receptionist?

No. ComFlow plays your greeting, records the voicemail, and processes it for your inbox. It does not hold a synthetic conversation or pretend to be a person. That boundary is intentional.

Can it make outbound calls?

The product supports tightly scoped scheduled calls: play a prepared message, ask one question, and make a best-effort capture of the answer. Outbound calling is off by default and is enabled only after a conversation with the team.
